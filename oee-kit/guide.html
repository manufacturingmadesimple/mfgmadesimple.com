Introduction & Preparation

Welcome to Your Fast-Track OEE Improvement Journey!

This kit is designed for manufacturing operations managers, plant managers, and production supervisors who need to demonstrate measurable improvement quickly. In just 5 days, you'll establish your baseline, identify opportunities, implement changes, and calculate ROI.

What You'll Achieve

By the end of this 5-day process, you will:

  • Know your current OEE performance with confidence
  • Identify 3-5 "quick win" improvements
  • Implement at least one meaningful change
  • Calculate the financial impact of your improvements
  • Build momentum for continuous improvement

Expected Results: Most manufacturers see 3-8 point OEE gains from quick wins alone, translating to thousands of dollars in monthly savings.

⏱️ Time Investment

  • Day 1: 2-3 hours (data collection + analysis)
  • Day 2: 1-2 hours (opportunity identification)
  • Day 3: 1 hour (action planning)
  • Day 4: 30 min/day (tracking + implementation)
  • Day 5: 1-2 hours (results calculation)

🛠️ What You'll Need

  • Production schedule/records
  • Downtime logs or access to operators
  • Calculator or this digital tool
  • Buy-in from ≥ 1 production supervisor
  • Optional: Stopwatch, Camera, Whiteboard

Day 1: Baseline Assessment (Monday)

Your Mission: Establish an honest, accurate baseline of your current OEE performance.

Step-by-Step Process

1. Choose Your Target Line/Cell (10 minutes)
Pick a production line that runs regularly (at least 3-4 days/week), has known quality issues or downtime, matters to your bottom line, and where you have the authority to change.

2. Gather Historical Data (30-60 minutes)
You need these 6 numbers from a typical shift or day:

Data Point Where to Find It Example
Total Scheduled Time Production schedule 480 min (8 hrs)
Planned Downtime Shift schedule (breaks, meetings) 30 min
Unplanned Downtime Downtime logs, supervisor notes 45 min
Ideal Cycle Time Engineering specs or time study 0.5 min/piece
Total Pieces Produced Production count sheets 750 pieces
Defective Pieces Quality logs (scrap + rework) 25 pieces

Pro Tips

  • Use actual data from last week, not theoretical "ideal" numbers.
  • If you don't track downtime formally, ask operators to keep a log for one shift.
  • Count rework as defects (it consumed time twice).
  • Be honest - you can't improve what you don't measure accurately.

3. Calculate Your Baseline OEE (5 minutes)
Enter your data into the tool and review your three factors:
• Availability = Time equipment was running vs. available time
• Performance = Actual output vs. theoretical maximum output
• Quality = Good parts vs. total parts produced
OEE = Availability × Performance × Quality

4. Identify Your Biggest Loss (15 minutes)
Which factor is lowest?
• Low Availability (<85%)? Focus on reducing downtime.
• Low Performance (<90%)? Focus on cycle time and minor stops.
• Low Quality (<95%)? Focus on defect reduction.
This tells you where to hunt for quick wins tomorrow.

Day 2: Quick Win Identification (Tuesday)

Your Mission: Find 3-5 low-effort, high-impact improvements you can implement this week.

The Quick Win Framework

Good quick wins have three characteristics:

  • Low effort: Can be done with existing resources in <1 week.
  • High impact: Will noticeably improve one of your OEE factors.
  • Sustainable: Won't require constant maintenance.

Where to Look (By OEE Factor)

⚠️ If Availability is Your Issue:

Look for: Frequent minor breakdowns, material waiting, long changeovers, operator waiting.

Common Quick Wins:

  • Implement 5S at changeover station
  • Create material staging area closer to line
  • Fix recurring mechanical issues (daily PM checklist)
  • Cross-train operators to eliminate shift gaps

⚙️ If Performance is Your Issue:

Look for: Micro-stops (jams, sensors), running below designed speed, lack of standard work, poor material presentation.

Common Quick Wins:

  • Adjust sensors to reduce false triggers
  • Document optimal settings & standardize
  • Improve part presentation at load point
  • Implement visual work instructions

✅ If Quality is Your Issue:

Look for: Defects caught late, inconsistent operator technique, tool wear/drift, poor incoming materials.

Common Quick Wins:

  • Add in-process check before value-add steps
  • Create go/no-go gauges at workstation
  • Implement poka-yoke (error-proofing) devices
  • Establish first-piece inspection ritual

Day 3: Action Planning (Wednesday)

Your Mission: Convert your quick wins into specific, actionable tasks with owners and deadlines.

Prioritization Matrix

For each opportunity found on Day 2, rate it using this matrix. Target: Identify 3-5 opportunities, with at least 2 rated "DO FIRST ⭐".

Low Effort High Effort
High Impact DO FIRST ⭐ Do Second
Low Impact Do Third Skip

Action Planning Template

For each selected quick win, define the following framework to ensure accountability:

  • What: Specific task (Example: "Install shadow board with labeled tool locations at Station 3")
  • Who: Single person accountable - not "team" (Example: "Production Supervisor John")
  • When: Specific deadline (Example: "Friday 3pm")
  • Resources: What's needed (Example: "Pegboard ($25), hooks ($15), labels, paint")

Implementation Sequence

Start with wins that:

  • Don't require capital approval
  • Can be done during normal shifts (no shutdown needed)
  • Have enthusiastic operator buy-in

Pro Tips

  • Get operators involved in the solution design.
  • Take "before" photos today.
  • Communicate the "why" to the team.
  • Set up a visual progress board if possible.

Day 4: Execute & Track (Thursday - Ongoing)

Your Mission: Implement improvements and monitor results daily.

Daily Tracking Ritual (15 minutes/day)

🌅 Morning Routine

  • Review yesterday's OEE metrics
  • Check status of action items
  • Brief team on what we're testing today

🌇 End of Shift Routine

  • Collect the same 6 data points as Day 1
  • Calculate daily OEE
  • Note: What changed? Worked? Didn't?
  • Capture operator feedback

What to Track (Daily Log)

Create a simple daily log to monitor the direct impact of your changes:

Date Availability Performance Quality OEE Notes
Mon (Baseline) 89.0% 87.5% 96.7% 75.3% Normal production
Tue 91.2% 87.5% 96.7% 77.2% Improved tool staging
Wed 91.2% 89.1% 96.7% 78.5% Standardized setup procedure

🎉 Celebrate Small Wins!

When you see improvement:

  • Share the results with the team
  • Thank people by name
  • Take "after" photos
  • Calculate the dollar impact (preview of Day 5)

Day 5: Results & ROI (Friday)

Your Mission: Quantify your improvement and build the business case for continued investment.

Step 1: Measure New OEE

Collect the same 6 data points from Day 1, using your best day from the week. Enter into the tool and compare Before vs. After.

Step 2: Calculate Financial Impact

The tool will calculate savings based on three core pillars:

  • Time Savings: Minutes of productive time gained per shift × Hourly labor cost = Labor cost savings per shift
  • Quality Savings: Number of defects eliminated × Material waste cost per defect = Material cost savings per shift
  • Capacity Gain: Additional good pieces produced = Revenue opportunity (if demand exists)

Step 3: Annualize the Impact

The ROI Formula

Your daily savings × 250 working days = Annual impact

Example: 3-point OEE gain
$150/shift savings × 250 days = $37,500/year

Step 4: Document & Share

Create a one-page summary with:

  • Before/After OEE (visual chart)
  • What We Changed (bullet points with photos)
  • Financial Impact (daily, monthly, annual)
  • Next Steps (what we'll tackle next)

Share with: Your team (celebrate!), Your boss (justify more resources), Other departments (spread the success).

Beyond Day 5: Sustaining & Scaling

Make It Stick

  • Week 2: Standardize the improvements (update work instructions), train all operators/shifts on new methods, continue daily tracking.
  • Week 3-4: Identify the next round of quick wins, start tackling medium-effort improvements, consider investing in better tracking tools.
  • Month 2-3: Apply this methodology to another line, train others to lead improvement projects, build toward world-class OEE (85%+).

Troubleshooting Common Challenges

"I don't have good data"
→ Start tracking manually for 3 days before beginning. A clipboard and stopwatch beat no data.

"My team is resistant to change"
→ Involve them in identifying problems. People support what they help create.

"I can't get time to implement"
→ Start with 30-minute improvements during shift changeover. Prove ROI, get more time.

"My OEE went down after changes"
→ Normal! New processes need adjustment. Give it 2-3 days before reverting.

"I found the problem but can't fix it without budget"
→ Document it. Use this data to justify capital in the next budget cycle.
